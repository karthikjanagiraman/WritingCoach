You've made a strong claim. Now let's support it. What evidence from the text backs up your argument?
E
The author says that recess helps kids focus
🦊
Good start! Can you be more specific? Quote the exact words and explain WHY they support your point.

Schools should extend recess to at least 30 minutes. According to a study by the CDC, students who have regular physical activity show "improved concentration and classroom behavior." When students return from a longer break, they're more focused and ready to learn. Some argue this wastes academic time, but the evidence shows it actually improves it.

Written by a Tier 2 student after 6 weeks
✨ Strong use of a counter-argument — addressing the other side makes your case even stronger

Live Lesson Preview
🐺
Your thesis about symbolism in The Giver is interesting, but it's too broad. How could you narrow it to focus on one specific symbol and its function in the narrative?
A
Maybe I could focus on how color represents lost individuality?
🐺
Excellent refinement. Now you have a focused lens. Let's outline how you'll build that argument across three body paragraphs.

In Lois Lowry's The Giver, the gradual emergence of color serves not merely as a plot device, but as a metaphor for the community's systematic suppression of individual experience. When Jonas first perceives the color red, Lowry signals the beginning of his awakening — a recognition that sameness, while safe, comes at the cost of everything that makes life meaningful.

Written by a Tier 3 student after 8 weeks
✨ Sophisticated analysis — connecting the specific (color red) to the thematic (cost of sameness)
